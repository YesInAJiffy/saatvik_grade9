

## **Robotics Glossary for High School Students**

### **Introduction**

Robotics combines mechanics, electronics, and programming to build machines that can move and perform tasks. Understanding these basic terms will help you design and build better robots.

***

### **Glossary Terms**

#### **Drivetrain**

*   **Definition**: The system that moves the robot by transferring power from motors to wheels or tracks.
*   **Example**: A robot with four wheels uses a drivetrain to make all wheels turn together.
*   **Related Terms**: Motor, Gear, Wheel, Axle.

***

#### **Motor**

*   **Definition**: A device that turns electrical energy into motion.
*   **Example**: DC motors spin wheels; servo motors move robot arms.
*   **Related Terms**: Torque, Controller, Battery.

***

#### **Gears**

*   **Definition**: Toothed wheels that mesh together to change speed or torque.
*   **Example**: Large gear driving a small gear makes the robot move faster.
*   **Related Terms**: Gear ratio, Torque.

***

#### **Bearings**

*   **Definition**: Parts that reduce friction so shafts or wheels can spin smoothly.
*   **Example**: Ball bearings in wheel hubs.
*   **Related Terms**: Axle, Chassis.

***

#### **Chassis**

*   **Definition**: The main frame that holds all robot parts together.
*   **Example**: Aluminum frame supporting motors and wheels.
*   **Related Terms**: Frame, Screws, Nuts.

***

#### **Screws**

*   **Definition**: Threaded fasteners used to hold parts together.
*   **Example**: Screws attach motors to the chassis.
*   **Related Terms**: Nuts, Bolts.

***

#### **Nuts**

*   **Definition**: Small metal pieces with threads that pair with screws or bolts.
*   **Example**: Nuts secure screws on the other side of a plate.
*   **Related Terms**: Screws, Washers.

***

#### **Torque**

*   **Definition**: The twisting force that makes things rotate.
*   **Example**: High torque motors lift heavy arms.
*   **Related Terms**: Motor, Gear ratio.

***

#### **Sprockets**

*   **Definition**: Wheels with teeth that grip a chain.
*   **Example**: Used in chain-driven drivetrains.
*   **Related Terms**: Chain, Gear.

***

#### **Pulleys**

*   **Definition**: Wheels that guide belts to transfer motion.
*   **Example**: Belt-driven arm uses pulleys.
*   **Related Terms**: Belt, Sprocket.

***

#### **Sensors**

*   **Definition**: Devices that detect information like distance, light, or touch.
*   **Example**: Ultrasonic sensor measures how far an object is.
*   **Related Terms**: Controller, Programming.

***

#### **Controllers**

*   **Definition**: The “brain” that runs the robot’s code and controls motors and sensors.
*   **Example**: Microcontroller like Arduino or VEX brain.
*   **Related Terms**: Programming, Battery.

***

#### **Wheels**

*   **Definition**: Round parts that allow the robot to roll.
*   **Example**: Rubber wheels for traction.
*   **Related Terms**: Axle, Drivetrain.

***

#### **Axles**

*   **Definition**: Rods that connect wheels and allow them to spin.
*   **Example**: Steel axle through two wheels.
*   **Related Terms**: Bearings, Chassis.

***

#### **Belts**

*   **Definition**: Flexible loops that transfer motion between pulleys.
*   **Example**: Belt-driven lift system.
*   **Related Terms**: Pulley, Chain.

***

#### **Chains**

*   **Definition**: Linked loops that transfer motion between sprockets.
*   **Example**: Chain-driven drivetrain.
*   **Related Terms**: Sprocket, Gear.

***

#### **Battery**

*   **Definition**: Stores electrical energy to power the robot.
*   **Example**: Rechargeable lithium battery.
*   **Related Terms**: Controller, Motor.

***

#### **Actuator**

*   **Definition**: A part that moves something, like a motor or piston.
*   **Example**: Linear actuator lifts an arm.
*   **Related Terms**: Motor, Torque.

***

#### **Encoder**

*   **Definition**: A sensor that measures rotation or position.
*   **Example**: Wheel encoder tracks distance traveled.
*   **Related Terms**: Motor, Controller.

***

#### **Programming**

*   **Definition**: Writing code to tell the robot what to do.
*   **Example**: Code that makes the robot stop when it sees an obstacle.
*   **Related Terms**: Controller, Sensors.

***

#### **Frame**

*   **Definition**: The structure that supports the robot.
*   **Example**: Aluminum frame for strength.
*   **Related Terms**: Chassis, Screws.

***

#### **Linkage**

*   **Definition**: Connected parts that transfer motion.
*   **Example**: Arm linkage moves a claw.
*   **Related Terms**: Actuator, Bearings.

***

***

### **Common Robotics Tips**

*   **Gear Ratios**: Big gear driving small gear = more speed, less torque. Small gear driving big gear = more torque, less speed.
*   **Battery Safety**: Always charge batteries properly and never short-circuit them.
*   **Programming Hints**: Start simple—test each motor and sensor before writing complex code.
*   **Troubleshooting**: Check connections first; loose wires cause most problems.
*   **Strong Chassis**: Use triangles or cross-bracing for strength.
