### Tips to Make These Rules Stick

*   **Agree together:** Review and adjust the times/limits as a family so your teen buys in.
*   **Post & visible:** Print and place where everyone can see.
*   **Model & remind:** Adults follow device rules at meals too.
*   **Use tech tools:** Enable Screen Time / Family Link / Focus modes to automate limits.




Here are some practical and balanced **home rules for a teenager** that promote responsibility, respect, and independence:

***

### ✅ **1. Respect and Communication**

*   Speak respectfully to everyone in the household.
*   No yelling, name-calling, or disrespectful language.
*   Inform parents where you are going and when you’ll be back.

***

### ✅ **2. School and Responsibilities**

*   Complete homework and school assignments before leisure activities.
*   Keep grades at an agreed-upon level (e.g., no failing grades).
*   Help with household chores (e.g., cleaning room, dishes, laundry).

***

### ✅ **3. Technology and Screen Time**

*   No phones during family meals.
*   Limit social media and gaming to agreed hours (e.g., after homework).
*   No devices in bedrooms after a certain time (e.g., 10 PM).

***

### ✅ **4. Curfew and Safety**

*   Weekday curfew: \[Set time, e.g., 9 PM].
*   Weekend curfew: \[Set time, e.g., 11 PM].
*   Always answer calls or texts from parents when out.

***

### ✅ **5. Friends and Visitors**

*   Ask permission before inviting friends over.
*   No visitors when parents are not home.
*   Respect common areas and clean up after guests.

***

### ✅ **6. Health and Well-being**

*   Eat meals at home unless discussed.
*   No skipping meals or eating only junk food.
*   Maintain personal hygiene daily.

***

### ✅ **7. Consequences**

*   Breaking rules = loss of privileges (phone, outings, etc.).
*   Repeated violations = stricter limits or extra chores.
