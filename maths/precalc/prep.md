## 1. Top Core Learning Resources

To teach yourself the concepts or fill in specific gaps from your syllabus, use these highly structured platforms:

* **Khan Academy (Precalculus Course):** This is the gold standard for self-paced study. It aligns almost perfectly with your PDF, featuring comprehensive modules on *Trigonometric Equations & Identities, Conic Sections, Matrices, and Vectors*. The instant feedback and video explanations make it highly efficient.
* **The Organic Chemistry Tutor (YouTube):** Despite the name, his math playlists are arguably the best on the internet for high schoolers. Search his channel for "Precalculus Review" or specific topics like *"Law of Sines and Cosines Ambiguous Case"* or *"Rational Functions Asymptotes and Holes."* He walks through problem-solving step-by-step.
* **OpenStax Precalculus Textbook:** If you prefer a traditional textbook structure, OpenStax offers a completely free, peer-reviewed Precalculus book online. It contains dense practice sections at the end of every chapter with answer keys. Chapters 5–8 will completely cover your trigonometry requirements.

## 2. High-Quality Practice Tests & Questions (PDFs)

Since you need to pass a formal placement exam, practicing with full-length collegiate and high school final exams is the best way to build stamina and identify weak spots.

* **The "MathGuy.US" Practice Archive:** * *What it is:* A massive repository of free, clean math PDFs.
* *Where to look:* Search for **"MathGuy.US PreCalculus"** online. They provide comprehensive chapter-by-chapter practice tests (like Polynomials, Composition of Functions, and Exponential Equations) complete with fully worked-out solution keys.


* **MCCKC Math 150 (Pre-Calculus Final Exam Review PDF):** * *What it is:* A comprehensive, public university final review packet that perfectly mirrors your honors syllabus.
* *Why it fits:* It contains exact practice problems for graphing rational functions' end behavior, solving complex log equations, factoring quadratic trigonometric equations, and calculating the angle between vectors using the dot product.


* **VCC & NYIT Placement Exam Samples:** * *What it is:* Free institutional placement exams designed exactly for students wanting to skip ahead to Calculus.
* *Where to look:* Search for **"VCC Math Precalculus Test Sample Questions PDF"** or **"NYIT Pre-Calculus Practice Problems PDF"**. These provide great, concise multiple-choice diagnostic drills to test your baseline speed.

### Additional Tips for Passing
- **Focus on your list**: Prioritize graphing (polynomials, rationals, trig), end behavior/asymptotes, trig identities/equations, and applications (word problems, vectors, conics). These are often heavily tested.
- **Practice actively**: Do problems daily—watch a video/explanation, then solve without notes, then check answers. Track mistakes in an error log.
- **Time yourself**: Simulate test conditions with mixed-topic practice.
- **Textbook supplements**: If your school uses a specific book, check for online resources or older editions. Barron's or Princeton Review Precalculus books are solid for extra problems (library or cheap used copies).

Start with Khan Academy to diagnose gaps, supplement with OpenStax for reading/examples, and drill worksheets from Kuta. This combination should thoroughly prepare you to pass and succeed in Calculus AB. Good luck—you've got this! If you need help with a specific topic, share more details.
