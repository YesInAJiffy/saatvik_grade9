Here are the **Rules of Exponents** ‚Äî clearly explained and organized so you can use them for algebra, logs, and simplifying expressions.

These work for any real numbers $$a, b$$ (except where restricted) and integers $$m, n$$.

***

# ‚úÖ **1. Product of Powers Rule**

When multiplying powers with the **same base**, **add** the exponents:

$$
a^m \cdot a^n = a^{m+n}
$$

**Example:**

$$
3^4 \cdot 3^2 = 3^{4+2} = 3^6
$$

***

# ‚úÖ **2. Quotient of Powers Rule**

When dividing powers with the **same base**, **subtract** the exponents:

$$
\frac{a^m}{a^n} = a^{m-n} \quad (a \ne 0)
$$

**Example:**

$$
\frac{5^7}{5^3} = 5^{7-3} = 5^4
$$

***

# ‚úÖ **3. Power of a Power Rule**

Raise a power to another power ‚Üí **multiply** the exponents:

$$
(a^m)^n = a^{mn}
$$

**Example:**

$$
(2^4)^3 = 2^{12}
$$

***

# ‚úÖ **4. Power of a Product Rule**

A power distributes over multiplication:

$$
(ab)^n = a^n b^n
$$

**Example:**

$$
(3x)^2 = 3^2 x^2 = 9x^2
$$

***

# ‚úÖ **5. Power of a Quotient Rule**

A power distributes over a division:

$$
\left(\frac{a}{b}\right)^n = \frac{a^n}{b^n} \quad (b\ne0)
$$

**Example:**

$$
\left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^3 = \frac{2^3}{5^3}=\frac{8}{125}
$$

***

# ‚úÖ **6. Zero Exponent Rule**

Any nonzero number to the power of 0 equals **1**:

$$
a^0 = 1 \quad (a\ne0)
$$

**Example:**

$$
7^0 = 1
$$

***

# ‚úÖ **7. Negative Exponent Rule**

A negative exponent means **reciprocal**:

$$
a^{-n} = \frac{1}{a^n} \quad (a\ne0)
$$

**Example:**

$$
3^{-4} = \frac{1}{3^4}
$$

***

# ‚úÖ **8. Fractional Exponent Rule**

A fractional exponent represents a **root**:

$$
a^{\frac{m}{n}} = (a^m)^{1/n}
$$

or

$$
a^{\frac{m}{n}} = \sqrt[n]{a^m}
$$

**Example:**

$$
16^{1/2} = 4, \qquad 8^{2/3} = (8^{1/3})^2 = 2^2 = 4
$$

***

# ‚≠ê Bonus: Useful Special Cases

### **a. $$a^{1/2}$$**

$$
a^{1/2} = \sqrt{a}
$$

### **b. $$a^{1/3}$$**

$$
a^{1/3} = \sqrt[3]{a}
$$

### **c. $$a^{-1}$$**

$$
a^{-1} = \frac{1}{a}
$$
